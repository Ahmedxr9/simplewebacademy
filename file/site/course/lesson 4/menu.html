Explanations

Lesson 4: Functions and Scope --Introduction to Functions: Functions are like mini-programs inside your program. They have a specific job, and you can reuse them whenever you need that job done. It's like having a robot friend that can do a task for you whenever you ask. --Scope in JavaScript: Scope is like the visibility of your robot friend. If your friend is inside your house, they can't see what's happening outside. Similarly, variables inside a function can't be seen outside of it. This lesson helps you understand where your variables can "see." --Parameters and Arguments: Imagine your function is a chef in a kitchen. Parameters are like the ingredients they need, and arguments are the actual ingredients you provide when calling the function. This lesson walks you through how to give the right ingredients to your functions. --Creating Functions: Creating functions is like writing a recipe. This lesson guides you through the process of declaring, defining, and calling functions. It's your guide to becoming the master chef of your code kitchen.
